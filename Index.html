how to use ymir
how does it work without internet?
your phone talks directly to phones nearby — no internet, no server, no middleman. like walkie talkies, but for information. range is roughly 50–100 metres.
how do i find food, water, or shelter?
look at the map. pins dropped by people near you will appear. tap a pin to see what it is. tap "navigate →" and use the small circular map in the corner as a compass — walk toward the arrow until you arrive.
how do i mark a place for others?
tap anywhere on the map where you want to place the pin. then tap the + button at the bottom. choose what type of place it is, give it a name only locals would understand, and tap "drop pin."
what names should i use for pins?
never use real street names or building names. use only names that people who live there already know — "the blue door", "abu khalid's place", "the school everyone knows." if this phone is ever found, those names should mean nothing to a stranger.
how do i tell people i'm safe?
tap the ◉ button at the bottom. choose "i am safe", "i am injured", or "i need help." it sends silently to everyone nearby with no sound.
how do i find a family member?
tap the ♡ button at the bottom. post their name and any details. everyone nearby will see it. if someone finds them, they can update the post.
how do i share my pins with someone nearby?
tap the ⇄ button. tap "show my pins as QR." show your screen to the other person. they open ymir, tap ⇄, tap "scan QR code", and point their camera at your screen. your pins transfer instantly with no internet.
what if someone checks my phone?
the app looks like a weather forecast to anyone who opens it. to get back in, tap the temperature three times quickly. if you need to erase everything immediately — press and hold the ymir ᛉ text at the top of the screen for 3 seconds. everything will be deleted instantly.
does anyone know i'm using this?
no. no account. no login. no data sent to any server. ymir does not know you exist. your government does not know you exist. we do not know you exist.
